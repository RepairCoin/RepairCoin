# How do I configure my no-show policy?

Set up automatic penalties for customers who repeatedly miss
appointments without cancelling, so reliable customers get priority
booking and chronic no-shows are deterred.

## When to do this

Right after you set your appointment hours, especially if you offer
appointment-only services where no-shows cost you the slot's revenue.
Most shops set this once and adjust thresholds occasionally.

## Steps

1. From the shop dashboard, open the **Settings** tab → **No-Show
   Policy** section.
2. Turn on **Enable No-Show Tracking** at the top. Tracking has to be
   on for any of the tier rules to apply.
3. Configure **Penalty Tiers**. Each tier triggers when a customer
   reaches a no-show count threshold:
   - **Tier 2 — Caution.** Set the no-show count that promotes a
     customer to Caution, and the **advance booking hours** required
     at this tier (e.g., must book 24h ahead).
   - **Tier 3 — Deposit Required.** Set the no-show count and the
     deposit amount the customer must pay upfront for future
     bookings.
   - **Tier 4 — Suspended.** Set the no-show count after which a
     customer cannot book at your shop at all without manual
     reinstatement.
4. Optional: configure email/SMS notifications per tier so customers
   know when their status changes and why.
5. Save your changes.

A customer's current tier is shown on their profile and on their
bookings, so you can see at a glance whether they need deposits or
advance booking.

## Common pitfalls

- **AI booking blocked unexpectedly.** When the AI Sales Agent offers
  slots, it respects your no-show policy — Caution customers won't
  see slots inside their advance-booking window, and Suspended
  customers can't book at all. If you expected a customer to be able
  to book but they can't, check their tier on their profile.
- **Tier thresholds too tight.** Setting Tier 2 at 1 no-show is
  aggressive — even reliable customers occasionally have emergencies.
  Most shops use 2–3 for Caution.
- **Forgot to mark a missed appointment as a no-show.** A booking
  doesn't auto-promote to no-show; you mark it manually after the
  scheduled time passes. Use the no-show button on the booking entry
  in your calendar.
