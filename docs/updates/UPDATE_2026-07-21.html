Engineering Update

UI Consistency Pass, Service Cards & Stripe Audit

Unified the app's dark theme across customer & shop screens, streamlined the service cards, and audited the full Stripe integration.

Date:  July 21, 2026 Status:  ✓ Implemented · lint-clean · pending commit Focus:  UI polish + payments review

Summary

A focused polish day: several screens were still rendering as bright white cards against the app's dark theme, so we brought them in line, then simplified the shop's service cards down to what actually matters. We also ran a full audit of the Stripe integration — code, config, webhooks, and a live connectivity check. All UI changes pass linting with zero errors and are ready for review.

1 Customer Home — Theme Consistency FIXED

On the customer dashboard's Overview, two large sections were rendering as bright white cards while everything around them was dark — they stood out as visually "off."

  • "Trusted Professionals in Every Service" and "Popular Services Near You" were converted to the app's dark card style, matching the surrounding panels.
  • Category tiles, service thumbnails, ratings, "See more" links, and the scroll arrows were all re-styled so they read clearly on the dark background.

2 Shop "Auto Sales & Booking" AI Panel — Theme Consistency FIXED

On the service create/edit screen, the entire AI Sales Assistant panel was a white card sitting right next to the dark "Live Preview" — a jarring mismatch.

  • Re-themed the whole cluster (the assistant panel, the customer-questions FAQ editor, and the AI-spend indicator) to the dark form style, while keeping the green accents so the AI feature still stands out as distinct.
  • Tone selector, sample-reply bubbles, FAQ inputs, tip boxes, and the spend chip were each adjusted for dark-mode contrast and legibility.

3 Service Cards — Simplified & Clearer NEW FIXED

The shop's service cards had a cluttered 2×2 grid of four look-alike buttons (Edit, Delete, Availability, Calendar), where even Delete highlighted the same color as the safe actions. We reworked the whole interaction:

  • Click the card to open the service — the card now navigates straight to the service page (replacing the old pop-up modal), so the whole card is the primary action.
  • Trimmed to Edit + Delete. Availability and Calendar were removed from the card because they already live inside the service page as tabs — no need to duplicate them.
  • Delete is now clearly destructive — a compact icon button that turns red on hover, set apart from Edit, with a hover tooltip and a subtle "deleting…" animation.
  • The Share QR Code action stays as the prominent primary button.
Why it matters: less visual noise, a clear primary action (open the service), and a delete that finally looks like a delete — fewer accidental clicks, cleaner cards.

4 Stripe Integration — Full Audit REVIEW

Reviewed the entire Stripe setup end-to-end — service code, environment config, webhook handling, and a live connectivity check. Core payments are healthy; a few configuration gaps were flagged for ops.

  • Healthy: Stripe is reachable (test mode), the monthly subscription price is valid, and the webhook signature/raw-body handling is wired correctly — the most common integration mistake is not present here.
  • Flagged for ops: the tier-specific price IDs (Starter / Growth / Business) and the Agency plan price aren't set locally, so tiers silently fall back to the legacy $500 price; the staging env file has no Stripe keys; and a TLS-verification override is enabled in the local env (dev-only, must not reach production).
Action: these are configuration / operations items (live keys + per-tier price IDs), not code bugs — they belong to the go-live checklist.

5 Manual Test Flow

How to verify everything above. Run the app locally (npm run dev — frontend on :3001, backend on :4000).

A · Sign Up & Login

  1. Open the app and choose Log In / Connect.
  2. New user: connect a fresh wallet → complete onboarding / role selection → land on the correct dashboard (customer vs. shop).
  3. Returning user: reconnect the same wallet → no re-onboarding, routed straight to your dashboard.
  4. Refresh to confirm the session persists; log out / switch account to confirm it fully clears.

B · Customer Home (change #1)

  1. Log in as a customer → Overview tab.
  2. Confirm "Trusted Professionals" and "Popular Services Near You" are now dark cards matching the rest of the page — no white boxes.
  3. Check tiles, thumbnails, ratings, and scroll arrows are all legible.

C · Service Creation + AI Panel (change #2)

  1. Log in as a shop (active subscription) → Services → Create Service.
  2. Fill name / price / duration / category and save → the new service appears in the list.
  3. Confirm the "Auto Sales & Booking" panel is a dark card (not white): toggle, tone buttons, reply preview, FAQ inputs, tip box, and spend chip.
  4. Toggle AI on/off, switch tones, and add an FAQ entry — no visual glitches.

D · Service Cards (change #3)

  1. On the Services tab, click a card → it navigates to the service page (no modal).
  2. Confirm the card now shows only Edit, Delete, and Share QR Code.
  3. Open a service → confirm Availability and Calendar are reachable as tabs inside it.
  4. Hover Delete → tooltip appears and it turns red; deleting shows the animation and removes the service.
  5. With a blocked / no-subscription shop, confirm actions show the "Operations are blocked" message instead of navigating.
