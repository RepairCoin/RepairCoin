# AI Business Assistant — What You Can Ask

Your dashboard has one built-in AI assistant. Talk to it in plain language
(type or use voice) and it can do all of this in a single conversation:

1. **Answer questions** about your business
2. **Take marketing actions** (draft, schedule, and send campaigns)
3. **Work with images** for your marketing
4. **Show you how to use the dashboard** (how-to / product help)

It follows a simple rhythm — **tells you what's happening → recommends a next
step → does it for you** — but it never sends, orders, or charges anything
without you tapping to confirm.

---

## 1. Know your business

### Revenue & sales
- See your revenue for any period, with the trend vs. the period before.
- Find your best-selling and highest-earning services.
- Check how many bookings you've had.

> Ask: *"How much did I make this week?"* · *"Is revenue up or down?"* ·
> *"What are my top services this month?"*

### Customers
- See your top customers by spend or number of visits.
- Understand your customer mix (loyalty tiers).
- Check repeat vs. new customers — how loyal your base is.
- See outstanding reward-token balances.

> Ask: *"Who are my best customers?"* · *"How many repeat customers do I have?"*

### Operations & patterns
- See cancellations and no-shows.
- Find your busiest and quietest times and days.
- See how much the AI assistant is doing for you.

> Ask: *"How bad are my no-shows?"* · *"When am I busiest?"*

### Inventory
- Get an at-a-glance view of your stock health.
- See what's low and needs reordering.
- Check how fast your stock moves and how your inventory value is trending.

> Ask: *"What do I need to restock?"* · *"How's my inventory looking?"*

### Quick rundowns
- Get a one-shot summary of how the business is doing — revenue trend, top
  service, customers who've drifted away and what they're worth, low stock, and
  your quietest upcoming day — with one clear recommendation.
- Ask what's slipping and it will flag where things are getting worse
  (response time, reviews, no-shows/cancellations) with likely reasons.

> Ask: *"How are we doing?"* · *"Give me a rundown."* · *"What am I doing wrong?"*

---

## 2. Run marketing campaigns

### Find your audience
- Describe who you want to reach in plain words and the assistant sizes the
  group and previews who's in it before you commit.

> Ask: *"How many customers haven't booked in 90 days?"* ·
> *"Target my top 100 by spend."* · *"Reach my frequent visitors."*

### Draft a campaign
- The assistant writes the email subject and message for your chosen audience
  and shows it on a card with a **Send** button — including a rough estimate of
  the revenue opportunity.

> Ask: *"Draft a win-back email for customers who haven't booked in 3 months."*

### Send now or schedule
- Send immediately, or pick a date and time to send it later.

> Ask: *"Schedule this for Friday at 9am."*

### Need ideas?
- Ask for campaign suggestions and it will propose a few angles to start from.

> Ask: *"What campaign should I run this week?"*

**You're always in control:** the assistant only ever *proposes* a campaign —
nothing goes out until you review the card and tap Send.

---

## 3. Images & visuals

*(Image creation/editing must be enabled for your shop. Once on, you can:)*

### Create a banner
- Generate a branded marketing image from a description, styled to your brand.

> Ask: *"Make a banner for a weekend phone-repair promo."*

### Edit an image
- Change an existing image with a simple instruction.

> Ask: *"Add a 20% OFF badge."* · *"Make the background darker."*

### Use your own photos
- Reuse a photo you've already uploaded (like your storefront photo) as a
  campaign banner.

> Ask: *"Use my storefront photo as the banner."*

### Attach a photo in chat
- Tap **Attach** to drop a photo into the conversation, then:
  - **Have it analyzed** — get a description, color palette, and theme ideas.
  - **Edit it** — modify it with a prompt.
  - **Turn it into a banner** — drop it straight into a campaign.

> Ask (after attaching): *"What theme fits this?"* · *"Add a sale badge to this."*
> · *"Use this as my email banner."*

Your images stay private to your shop.

---

## 4. Get help using the dashboard

Not sure how to do something? Just ask — the assistant knows the product and
answers "how do I…" / "where do I…" questions about running your dashboard,
without you hunting through menus or a help center.

- How to set up and manage your services, hours, and bookings.
- Where to find a setting or feature.
- How a feature works and how to use it.

> Ask: *"How do I create a service?"* · *"Where do I set my operating hours?"* ·
> *"How do I block a customer?"* · *"How do refunds work?"*

---

## 5. Good to know

- **Plain language works** — you don't need exact wording or menus; just ask.
- **It uses your real data** — every number comes from your own shop's records,
  scoped only to you. It won't make figures up; if there's no data, it says so.
- **You confirm every action** — drafts, sends, scheduled sends, restock orders,
  and image changes all wait for your tap. The assistant never acts on its own.
- **Voice or text** — ask by typing or by talking.

### One conversation, start to finish
A typical flow looks like this:

> **You:** "How are we doing?"
> **Assistant:** *gives the rundown* — "Revenue's down this week; you have a few
> customers who haven't booked in a while worth winning back."
> **You:** "Do it."
> **Assistant:** *sizes the audience, drafts a win-back email with a revenue
> estimate, and shows it with a Send button.*
> **You:** "Add a banner with our storefront photo, then schedule it for Friday."

…all without leaving the chat.
