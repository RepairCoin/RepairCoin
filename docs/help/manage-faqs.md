# How do I manage FAQs for a service?

Add or edit the FAQ entries the AI Sales Agent quotes from when
answering customer questions about a specific service.

## When to do this

Whenever you notice customers asking the same question repeatedly, or
when you want to make sure the AI gives a consistent, accurate answer
to a tricky question (cancellation policy, parking, what to bring).

## Steps

1. Open the service detail page → **AI Assistant** tab.
2. Scroll to the **FAQs** section inside the Auto Sales & Booking card.
3. To add or edit entries:
   - Type the customer's likely question in the **question** field.
   - Type your answer in the **answer** field. Keep it short and
     specific — one to three sentences.
4. To get suggestions, click **Suggest questions with AI**. The AI
   reads your service description and proposes 5 likely customer
   questions with hint text on what to answer. Fill in the answers
   in your own words.
5. To paste in source material the AI can answer from, expand **Add
   source material** and paste relevant text (policies, FAQs from
   another site, etc.). The AI uses this when generating suggestions.
6. Use the up/down arrows to reorder entries, or the trash icon to
   remove one.
7. Click **Save Changes** in the sticky bar at the bottom.

## Common pitfalls

- **Don't add price or duration questions.** The AI already knows
  your service price and duration from the structured fields. FAQ
  entries for those override the real values if they disagree — easy
  way to drift out of sync. Skip them.
- **Empty starter questions don't persist.** The editor seeds a
  starter question to give you a template. Starter rows with blank
  answers are stripped on save — they won't show up next reload.
- **Long answers get expensive and dilute attention.** The AI quotes
  your entries in chat replies; lengthy answers make replies feel
  robotic. Aim for ~2 sentences per answer.
- **AI ignores an FAQ entry.** If a customer asks a question and the
  AI answers from the service description instead of your FAQ, your
  FAQ question phrasing might not match what the customer actually
  typed. Try rewording the question to match the most likely customer
  phrasing.
